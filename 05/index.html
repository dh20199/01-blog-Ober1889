Archduke Charles Von Teschen Fieldmarshal of Austria

Author: Christopher Obermoser

Biographical Information

Archduke Charles Von Teschen is considered to be one of the most influencial military of the late 18th and early 19th ceentury. Brother to Emperor Francis of Austria, Archduke Charles was born in Florence in 1771; however, he spent much of his early life in the Austrian Neatherlands. He is most famous for his military reforms, and for having been the first coalition general to defeat Napoleon Bonaparte in battle.


Important Battles between the French and Habsburg Armies

  • Battle of Caldiero (30 Oct 1805)
  • Battle of Austerlitz (2 Dec 1805)
  • Battle of Aspern-Essling (21-22 MAy 1809)
  • Battle of Wagram (5-6 July 1809)


Battle of Aspern-Essling

In the wake of the disastrous Battle of Austerlitz on the Pratzen Heights and the colapse of the Third Coalition, it became apparent that there was an urgent requirement to put aside political agendas and install competent leader at the head of the Imperial Army. In lightt of Archduke Charles's recent successful campaingn in Italy, he was chosen as Fieldmarshall. He embarked on an agenda of military reforms, which were put to the test in 1809 when he met Napoleon Bonaparte at the Battle of Aspern-Essling.

The Battle of Aspern-Essling took place from 21 to 22 MAy of 1809, represented a significant moment in history; Napoleon Bonaparte's first defeat. After two days of brutal fighting in the towns of Aspern and Essling and surounding terrain, Archduke Charle's reformed army proved itself by staunchly defeating Napoleon's Grand Armee. The battle was hard fought by both sides, filled with moments of peril, and displys of great courage. It was a historic moment, as the world had come to realize that Napoleon Bonaparte could be defeated.
